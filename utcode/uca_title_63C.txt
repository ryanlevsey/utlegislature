Utah Code Annotated - Title 63C - Chapter 1 - General Provisions
****************************************************************

Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-101 Title.
--------------------------------------
This title is known as the "State Commissions and Councils Code."

Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102 Definitions.
--------------------------------------------
As used in this title:Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(1): "Advisory board," "advisory commission," and "advisory council" means a board, commission, or council that:
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(1)(a): provides advice and makes recommendations to another person or entity who makes policy for the benefit of the general public;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(1)(b): is created by and whose duties are provided by statute or by executive order; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(1)(c): performs its duties only under the supervision of another person as provided by statute.
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(2): "Review board," "review commission," or "review council" means a board, commission, or council that:
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(2)(a): possesses a portion of the sovereign power of the state only to the extent to enable it to approve policy made for the benefit of the general public by another body or person;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(2)(b): is created by and whose duties are provided by statute;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(2)(c): performs its duties according to its own rules without supervision other than under the general control of another person as provided by statute; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(2)(d): is permanent and continuous and not temporary and occasional.
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(3): "Policy board," "policy commission," or "policy council" means a board, commission, or council that:
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(3)(a): possesses a portion of the sovereign power of the state to enable it to make policy for the benefit of the general public;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(3)(b): is created by and whose duties are provided by the constitution or by statute;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(3)(c): performs its duties according to its own rules without supervision other than under the general control of another person as provided by statute; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-1-102(3)(d): is permanent and continuous and not temporary and occasional.

Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-103 Policy for public lands within the state.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
It is the policy of the state to claim and preserve by lawful means the rights of the state and its citizens to determine and affect the disposition and use of federal lands within the state as those rights are granted by the United States Constitution, the Utah Enabling Act, and other applicable law.

Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204 Review of presidential executive orders.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(1): The council shall review certain executive orders by the president of the United States that are not affirmed by a vote of the United States Congress and signed into law as prescribed by the Constitution of the United States.
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(2): Upon review, the council may recommend to the attorney general and the governor that an executive order be further examined by the attorney general to determine:
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(2)(a): the constitutionality of the executive order; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(2)(b): whether the state should seek to have the executive order declared to be an unconstitutional exercise of legislative authority by the president.
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3): Notwithstanding any other provision of law, no state agency, political subdivision, elected or appointed state official or employee, or official or employee of a political subdivision may implement a presidential executive order that is determined by the attorney general to be unconstitutional under this section if the order relates to:
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3)(a): a pandemic or other public health emergency;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3)(b): the regulation of natural resources;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3)(c): the regulation of the agricultural industry;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3)(d): the regulation of land use;
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3)(e): the regulation of the financial sector through the imposition of environmental, social, or governance standards; or
Utah Code Annotated § 63C-4a-204(3)(f): the regulation of the constitutional right to keep and bear arms.
